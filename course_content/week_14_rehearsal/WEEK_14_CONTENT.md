## Content to Create

### Video 1: Delivery Techniques (8 min)

**Key Topics:**
- Voice: Pace, volume, clarity
  - Slow down (nerves speed you up)
  - Pause after key points
  - Vary tone to maintain interest

- Body Language:
  - Eye contact (look at audience, not screen)
  - Gestures (natural, purposeful)
  - Posture (confident, open)
  - Movement (deliberate, not pacing)

- Slides as Support:
  - Don't read slides
  - Glance, don't stare
  - Use as visual cues, not scripts

- Team Transitions:
  - "Now Sarah will walk us through elasticity..."
  - Physical handoff (move positions)
  - Maintain attention during others' sections

**Animation Ideas:**
- Split screen: Good vs bad delivery examples
- Checklist appearing

---

### Video 2: Common Pitfalls (5 min)

**Key Topics:**
- Reading slides verbatim
- Going over time
- Rushing through important points
- Not making eye contact
- Filler words ("um," "like," "you know")
- Technical issues (have backups!)
- Not anticipating questions
- Team coordination issues

**How to Avoid:**
- Practice out loud (not just in your head)
- Time each section
- Record yourself and watch
- Prepare backup slides
- Anticipate 10 likely questions

---

### Video 3: Recording Tips (5 min)

**Key Topics:**
- Why record a rehearsal: See yourself as others do
- Setup: Good lighting, clear audio, visible slides
- What to review:
  - Timing by section
  - Filler words count
  - Eye contact
  - Energy level
  - Team transitions

- Tools: Zoom recording, Loom, phone on tripod

### One-Page Executive Summary Template
**File:** `executive_summary_onepage_template.md`

```markdown
# AWG Pricing Analysis: Executive Summary

## The Challenge
AWG sought to understand if their private label brands (Best Choice, Always Save) are competitively priced against national brands, and how various factors affect their pricing strategy.

## What We Did
Analyzed [X] products across [X] categories and [X] divisions over 4 quarters, examining price gaps, cost pass-through, demand elasticity, cross-brand dynamics, geographic variation, and tariff impacts.

## Key Findings

| Area | Finding | Implication |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Price Gap | 32% average (exceeds 20% target) | Competitive positioning strong |
| Pass-Through | AWG absorbs 65% of cost changes | Protecting retailers from volatility |
| Elasticity | Demand mostly inelastic (ε = -0.28) | Modest price increases feasible |
| Cross-Brand | BC/AS serve different segments | Minimal cannibalization risk |
| Geographic | Pricing consistent (1.9% CV) | Uniform strategy working |
| Tariffs | Sensitive categories show 2x cost increase | Monitor and manage |

## Top Recommendations

1. **[Recommendation 1]**: [Specific action and expected impact]
2. **[Recommendation 2]**: [Specific action and expected impact]
3. **[Recommendation 3]**: [Specific action and expected impact]

## Next Steps
- [Immediate action item]
- [Further analysis suggested]
- [Implementation consideration]

---
*Prepared by [Team Name] | University of Illinois | Spring 2026*
```

### Q&A Preparation Sheet
**File:** `qa_preparation_sheet.md`

```markdown
# Anticipated Questions & Prepared Responses

## Methodology Questions

**Q: Why did you use [methodology]?**
A: [Prepared response]

**Q: How confident are you in these numbers?**
A: [Prepared response with confidence intervals/caveats]

## Data Questions

**Q: What about the unit normalization - are you sure BSP is case-level?**
A: [Prepared response with evidence]

**Q: Why do some products have negative price gaps?**
A: [Prepared response]

## Business Questions

**Q: What would you recommend we do first?**
A: [Priority recommendation with rationale]

**Q: How much revenue impact could these recommendations have?**
A: [Prepared response or acknowledge limitation]

## Challenge Questions

**Q: I'm not sure I agree with [finding]. Can you explain?**
A: [Non-defensive response, offer to dive deeper]

**Q: What didn't you analyze that you wish you had?**
A: [Honest response about limitations]
```
