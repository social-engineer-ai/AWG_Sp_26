# Week 12: Integration & Synthesis

## Overview
**Theme:** Putting It All Together
**Duration:** Twelfth week of semester
**Deliverables:** Integrated Findings Document, Cross-RQ Insights, Draft Recommendations

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, students will be able to:
1. Connect findings across all 6 research questions
2. Identify overarching themes and patterns
3. Develop actionable strategic recommendations
4. Prioritize insights by business impact

## Content to Create

### Video 1: Connecting Findings (10 min)

**Key Topics:**
- RQ1-6 are not separate stories - they're one story
- The narrative thread:
  ```
  RQ1: BC is priced competitively (32% gap)
       ↓
  RQ2: AWG absorbs costs to maintain this (35% pass-through)
       ↓
  RQ3: Demand is inelastic - can support prices (ε = -0.28)
       ↓
  RQ4: BC and AS serve different segments (no cannibalization)
       ↓
  RQ5: Pricing is consistent across divisions (1.9% CV)
       ↓
  RQ6: Tariff-sensitive categories show higher cost pressure
  ```
- Synthesis: Sustainable competitive pricing strategy, but category-level opportunities exist

**Animation Ideas:**
- Web diagram connecting all 6 RQs
- Story flow animation

### Video 2: Strategic Recommendations (8 min)

**Key Topics:**
- Moving from analysis to action
- Recommendation framework: Observation → Insight → Action
- Priority matrix: Impact vs Feasibility

**Example Recommendations:**
1. **Price Gap Optimization**
   - Categories below 20%: Consider reducing BC prices
   - Categories well above: Opportunity to capture margin

2. **Pass-Through Strategy**
   - Monitor cost changes closely
   - Consider differentiated response by brand

3. **Elasticity-Based Pricing**
   - Inelastic categories: Modest price increases possible
   - Elastic categories: Hold or reduce prices

4. **Geographic Consistency**
   - Investigate List Cost variation
   - Consider division-specific strategies where warranted

**Animation Ideas:**
- Priority matrix with recommendations plotted
- Recommendation cards with actions

---

### Video 3: Executive Summary Writing (7 min)

**Key Topics:**
- Pyramid principle revisited
- One-page format: Situation → Findings → Recommendations
- Writing for executives: No jargon, clear numbers, so-what emphasis
- Formatting tips: Bullets, bold key numbers, white space

**Template Structure:**
```
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

SITUATION
AWG asked us to evaluate private label pricing strategy...

KEY FINDINGS
• Price Gap: 32% average (exceeds 20% target)
• Cost Pass-Through: AWG absorbs 65% of cost changes
• Demand: Mostly inelastic (median ε = -0.28)
• Brand Dynamics: BC and AS serve different segments
• Geographic: Consistent pricing across divisions
• Tariffs: Evidence of impact on sensitive categories

RECOMMENDATIONS
1. [Specific action] - [Expected impact]
2. [Specific action] - [Expected impact]
3. [Specific action] - [Expected impact]

NEXT STEPS
[What should happen after this project]
```

## Notes

Week 12 is about synthesis - the hardest analytical skill. Students often struggle to move from "what we found" to "what it means." Key messages:
1. The client cares about decisions, not just findings
2. Recommendations must be specific and actionable
3. Connect dots that aren't obvious
4. Write for busy executives
