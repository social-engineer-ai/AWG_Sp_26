# Week 2: Client Introduction & Project Kickoff

## Overview
**Theme:** Meet AWG
**Duration:** Second week of semester
**Deliverables:** Team Charter, Initial Questions for Client

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, students will be able to:
1. Explain AWG's business model and private label strategy

## Content to Create

### Video 1: AWG Company Background (5 min)
**Purpose:** Help students understand the client and their business context

**Script Outline:**

**[INTRO - 30 sec]**
"Before we dive into data analysis, we need to understand our client. Who is AWG, and why does this project matter to them?"

**[SECTION 1: Who is AWG? - 1 min]**
- Associated Wholesale Grocers
- Largest retailer-owned cooperative grocery wholesaler in the US
- Founded in 1924, headquartered in Kansas City
- Serves 3,000+ independently owned grocery stores across 28 states
- Annual sales: ~$11 billion

**Animation:** Map of US showing AWG's coverage area, highlighting the 9 divisions

**[SECTION 2: The Business Model - 1 min]**
- Cooperative structure: Member stores own AWG
- AWG negotiates bulk purchasing power
- Stores get competitive pricing + support services
- Different from corporate chains (Kroger, Walmart)

**Animation:** Diagram showing flow: Suppliers → AWG → Member Stores → Consumers

**[SECTION 3: Private Label Strategy - 1.5 min]**
- What are private labels? Store brands vs national brands
- AWG's brands:
  - **Best Choice** - Quality comparable to national brands, lower price
  - **Always Save** - Value-focused, budget-conscious shoppers
- Why private labels matter:
  - Higher margins for retailers
  - Customer loyalty (can only get these at AWG stores)
  - Competitive differentiation

**Animation:** Product comparison showing National Brand cereal vs Best Choice vs Always Save with price points

**[SECTION 4: The Challenge - 1 min]**
- How should AWG price their private labels?
- Too high → customers choose national brands
- Too low → leaving money on the table
- Target: Best Choice should be ~20% below national brand equivalent
- But is this target being met? Does it vary by category? By region?

**Animation:** Price gap visual with 20% target line

**[OUTRO - 30 sec]**
"This is where you come in. AWG has given us real pricing and sales data to answer these questions. Your analysis will directly inform their pricing strategy."

### Video 2: Research Questions Overview (8 min)
**Purpose:** Deep dive into each of the 6 research questions

**Script Outline:**

**[INTRO - 30 sec]**
"AWG wants to understand their pricing strategy from multiple angles. We'll tackle 6 interconnected research questions over the semester."

**[RQ1: Price Gap Analysis - 1 min]**
"Are AWG private labels priced competitively against national brands?"
- The 20% target: BC should be 20% cheaper than national equivalent
- Key metrics: Price gap percentage, variance by category
- Why it matters: Core competitive positioning

**Animation:** Price comparison visual with gap calculation

**[RQ2: Cost Pass-Through - 1 min]**
"When supplier costs change, how much passes through to retail prices?"
- If AWG's cost increases $1, does price increase $1? More? Less?
- Compare: National brands vs Best Choice vs Always Save
- Why it matters: Margin protection, competitive response

**Animation:** Flow diagram showing cost → wholesale → retail with pass-through percentages

**[RQ3: Price Elasticity - 1 min]**
"How sensitive is demand to price changes?"
- Elasticity = % change in quantity / % change in price
- Elastic products: small price change → big sales change
- Inelastic products: price changes don't affect sales much
- Why it matters: Which products can support price increases?

**Animation:** Two demand curves - elastic (flat) vs inelastic (steep)

**[RQ4: Cross-Brand Effects - 1 min]**
"Do Best Choice and Always Save compete with each other?"
- Cross-elasticity: Does BC price change affect AS sales?
- Substitutes vs complements
- Why it matters: Avoid cannibalization, optimize portfolio

**Animation:** Two-brand diagram showing potential customer switching

**[RQ5: Geographic Variation - 1.5 min]**
"Does pricing strategy vary across AWG's 9 divisions?"
- Divisions: Kansas City, Springfield, Oklahoma, etc.
- Urban vs rural pricing
- Regional cost differences
- Why it matters: Should pricing be uniform or localized?

**Animation:** Map with division highlighting, price index comparison

**[RQ6: Tariff Impact - 1 min]**
"How have tariffs affected pricing and costs?"
- Tariff-sensitive categories: coffee, oils, imported goods
- Did costs increase? Did prices follow?
- Why it matters: External shock response, future planning

**Animation:** Timeline showing tariff implementation and price/cost changes

**[CONNECTIONS - 30 sec]**
"These questions aren't isolated. RQ1 findings inform RQ4. RQ2 connects to RQ6. By the end, you'll weave these into a complete pricing strategy story."

**Animation:** Web diagram connecting all 6 RQs

**[OUTRO - 30 sec]**
"Over the next 13 weeks, you'll tackle each of these systematically. First up: understanding the data."

### Video 4: Team Charter Guide (5 min)
**Purpose:** Help teams establish effective working agreements

**Script Outline:**

**[INTRO - 30 sec]**
"Great analysis requires great teamwork. This week, you'll create a team charter - a shared agreement for how you'll work together."

**[SECTION 1: Why Team Charters? - 1 min]**
- Prevents misunderstandings later
- Sets expectations early
- Creates accountability
- Reference point when things get difficult

**[SECTION 2: Key Components - 2.5 min]**

**Roles:**
- Team Lead: Coordinates, runs meetings, liaison with instructor
- Technical Lead: Code quality, notebook organization
- Research Lead: Literature, methodology rigor
- Communication Lead: Client-facing materials, presentations
- Note: Roles can rotate, everyone codes

**Communication:**
- Primary channel (Slack? Discord? GroupMe?)
- Response time expectations
- Meeting schedule (when, where, how long)

**Working Agreements:**
- How decisions are made
- What "done" means for deliverables
- How to handle disagreements
- What happens if someone misses a deadline

**[SECTION 3: Tips for Success - 1 min]**
- Be specific, not vague
- Discuss uncomfortable scenarios now
- Review and revise mid-semester
- Document everything

**[OUTRO - 30 sec]**
"Complete your team charter by the end of this week. It's your first deliverable - and your foundation for everything that follows."
