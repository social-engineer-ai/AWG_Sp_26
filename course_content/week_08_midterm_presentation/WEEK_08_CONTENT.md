# Week 8: Mid-Term Presentation to Client

## Overview
**Theme:** Progress Check-In
**Duration:** Eighth week of semester
**Deliverables:** Mid-Term Slides, Executive Summary (RQ1-3), Client Questions

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, students will be able to:
1. Synthesize analytical findings into a coherent narrative
2. Create client-ready presentation materials
3. Present technical findings to non-technical audiences
4. Handle Q&A professionally
5. Gather feedback to refine remaining analysis

---

## Presentation Requirements

**Duration:** 20-25 minutes + 10-15 minutes Q&A

**Structure:**
1. Data Overview & Quality (5 min)
2. RQ1: Price Gap Analysis (5 min)
3. RQ2: Cost Pass-Through (5 min)
4. RQ3: Price Elasticity (5 min)
5. Questions for Client (3 min)
6. Next Steps: RQ4-6 Preview (2 min)

---

## Content to Create

### Video 1: Presentation Structure (8 min)
**Purpose:** How to organize findings for client impact

**Script Outline:**

**[INTRO - 30 sec]**
"You've done seven weeks of analysis. Now you need to make it matter. A great presentation tells a story, not just reports numbers."

---

**[SECTION 1: Start with the Answer - 2 min]**

**Wrong Approach:**
"First we loaded the data, then we cleaned it, then we melted it, then we calculated..."

**Right Approach:**
"Best Choice is competitively priced - 32% below national brands on average, exceeding the 20% target. Here's how we know."

**Animation:** Pyramid principle diagram

**The Pyramid:**
```
          [ANSWER]
         /   |   \
      [Support] [Support] [Support]
       /|\       /|\        /|\
    [Details] [Details] [Details]
```

"Lead with conclusions. Support with evidence. Save methodology for appendix."

---

**[SECTION 2: One Slide, One Message - 2 min]**

**Bad Slide:**
- 6 bullet points
- 2 charts
- Dense paragraph
- No clear takeaway

**Good Slide:**
- Clear title stating the insight
- One supporting visual
- 3-4 key data points
- White space

**Animation:** Before/after slide transformation

**Title Guidelines:**
- Bad: "Price Gap Analysis Results"
- Good: "Best Choice Exceeds 20% Target in 78% of Products"

---

**[SECTION 3: Data Quality First - 1.5 min]**

"Before showing results, establish credibility."

**Key Points:**
1. What data we analyzed (files, rows, time period)
2. What issues we found and how we resolved them
3. The unit discovery (BSP case vs SRP unit)
4. Remaining questions for client

**Animation:** Data quality scorecard

"Show the client: We were thorough. We found problems. We fixed what we could."

---

**[SECTION 4: Visual Hierarchy - 2 min]**

**For Each RQ:**
1. **Headline:** One sentence answer
2. **Key Visual:** Chart that proves the point
3. **Supporting Numbers:** 2-3 key statistics
4. **So What?:** Business implication

**Animation:** Slide template with sections labeled

**Example - RQ1:**
```
Title: "Best Choice Is Competitively Priced (32% Average Gap)"

[Histogram of price gaps with 20% target line]

Key Numbers:
• Average gap: 32.3%
• Median gap: 31.5%
• 78% of products ≥20% target

Insight: Target exceeded overall, but specific categories
like Oral Hygiene (12%) need attention.
```

---

**[OUTRO - 30 sec]**
"Your presentation should pass the 'squint test' - if someone squints at your slide, they should still get the main point."

### Video 2: Data Storytelling (10 min)
**Purpose:** Turning analysis into narrative

**Script Outline:**

**[INTRO - 30 sec]**
"Data tells you what happened. Stories tell you what it means. The best analysts are storytellers."

---

**[SECTION 1: The Story Arc - 2.5 min]**

**Every story has:**
1. **Setting:** The context (AWG, private labels, competitive pricing)
2. **Conflict:** The question (Are we competitively priced?)
3. **Journey:** The analysis (What we did, what we found)
4. **Resolution:** The answer (Yes, with caveats)
5. **Implications:** The "so what" (Recommendations)

**Animation:** Story arc curve

**For Mid-Term:**
```
Setting: AWG wants Best Choice 20% below national brands
Conflict: Is this happening? How do costs flow to prices? Is demand sensitive?
Journey: We analyzed pricing, calculated gaps, estimated pass-through and elasticity
Resolution: Gap exceeds target (32%), pass-through varies by brand, most products inelastic
Implications: Opportunities in specific categories, pricing strategy validated
```

---

**[SECTION 2: Connecting Findings - 2.5 min]**

"RQ1, RQ2, RQ3 aren't separate. They're connected."

**The Connection:**
```
RQ1 (Price Gap: 32%)
     ↓
"But can we sustain this gap when costs change?"
     ↓
RQ2 (Pass-Through: BC absorbs more cost than NB)
     ↓
"Does pricing affect sales?"
     ↓
RQ3 (Elasticity: Most products inelastic)
     ↓
SYNTHESIS: BC is competitively priced, absorbs costs,
and demand is stable - sustainable strategy
```

**Animation:** Connecting arrows between RQ boxes

---

**[SECTION 3: The "So What" Test - 2 min]**

**For Every Slide, Ask:**
- Why does the client care?
- What should they do differently?
- What decision does this inform?

**Bad:** "Pass-through rate is 0.35"
**Good:** "AWG absorbs 65% of cost increases for Best Choice, protecting retailers from price volatility"

**Bad:** "Elasticity is -0.28"
**Good:** "Most products can support modest price increases without significant sales loss"

**Animation:** "So What?" thought bubble

---

**[SECTION 4: Acknowledging Limitations - 1.5 min]**

"Confidence comes from honesty about uncertainty."

**What to Acknowledge:**
- Data limitations (only 4 quarters, specific divisions)
- Methodology simplifications (endogeneity in elasticity)
- Questions that remain (List Cost variation, data corrections)

**How to Frame:**
"Our analysis assumes... Further work could..."
NOT: "This might be wrong because..."

**Animation:** Confidence interval visual

---

**[SECTION 5: Questions for Client - 1 min]**

"Mid-term is two-way. You need answers to continue."

**Good Questions:**
- Can you confirm BSP is case-level, SRP is unit-level?
- Why does List Cost vary across divisions?
- Are these 40 negative-gap products correctly linked?
- Any categories we should prioritize for deep-dive?

---

**[OUTRO - 30 sec]**
"A good mid-term makes the client excited about the final. Make them want to see RQ4-6."

---

### Video 3: Handling Q&A (5 min)
**Purpose:** Professional responses to client questions

**Script Outline:**

**[INTRO - 30 sec]**
"Q&A can make or break a presentation. Preparation is key."

---

**[SECTION 1: Anticipate Questions - 1.5 min]**

**Common Client Questions:**
- "How confident are you in these numbers?"
- "Why did you choose that methodology?"
- "What about [category/product] I care about?"
- "How does this compare to our expectations?"

**Prepare Short Answers + Backup Slides**

**Animation:** Question cards appearing

---

**[SECTION 2: Answer Techniques - 2 min]**

**The PREP Method:**
- **P**oint: Direct answer first
- **R**eason: Why you believe this
- **E**xample: Specific supporting evidence
- **P**oint: Restate the answer

**Animation:** PREP framework

**When You Don't Know:**
"That's a great question. We didn't analyze that specifically, but based on what we've seen... [hypothesis]. We can investigate further."

NEVER: "I don't know" (alone)
ALWAYS: "I don't know, but I can find out" or "Here's what we DO know that's related..."

---

**[SECTION 3: Difficult Situations - 1 min]**

**If challenged on methodology:**
"We chose this approach because... An alternative would be... We can compare approaches if helpful."

**If asked about something not in scope:**
"Great question - that's on our radar for RQ4-6. [OR] That's beyond our current scope but we could add it."

**If the client disagrees:**
"Help me understand your concern..." (don't be defensive)

---

**[OUTRO - 30 sec]**
"Q&A is where trust is built. Be confident, be humble, be helpful."
